Introduction through a laboratory incident

Laboratory accidents do happen and laboratory infection could happen, so introduction through a laboratory incident is at least possible.

The Wuhan CDC laboratory moved on 2nd December 2019, which might have led to a laboratory incident. However, there were no incidents caused by the lab move.

The fact that the CoV RaTG13 strain was sequenced at the Wuhan Institute of Virology suggests the possibility of introduction through a laboratory incident, but the closest relatives of SARS-CoV-2 are evolutionarily distant.

The high quality biosafety at laboratories, the limited number of samples processed, and absence of any record of viruses closely related to SARS-CoV-2 in any laboratory each contradict the hypothesis of Introduction through a laboratory incident.
