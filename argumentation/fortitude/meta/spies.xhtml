Notes

In the Fortitude South example we have consolidated all reports from agents into a single item of SPIES evidence that is used in ACH to support the CALAIS hypothesis. This is very much a "big picture" argument that you might build up from a lot of detailed arguments about individual agent reports and what they mean.

The agents aren't in a position to know specific Allied plans, but they are in a position to report Allied troop movements in England. The SPIES claim is effectively the product of a complex intelligence process that has agent reporting as its input. To say, as we have here, that agents are asserting the claim made by the intelligence product is something of a stretch, but useful shorthand nevertheless. Arguments are there to be challenged and questioned, and this approach provides hooks for some mechanism to "drill down" into the details.
