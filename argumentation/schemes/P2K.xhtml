Position to Know

This scheme asserts that an unsupported CLAIM in an argument map is made by a speaker in a position to know the truth of the matter. The DATA for the scheme is the observation that prompts the claim. The WARRANT is the assumption that the speaker would know the truth and report it faithfully, and the QUALIFIER is that the speaker is presumably reliable.

The inference is an example of the Argument from Position to Know scheme for presumptive reasoning. This defines a Lack of Reliability exception.

The critical questions are:

  • Is the claim from someone who is in a position to know?

  • Can the person making the claim be trusted to report honestly and accurately?

Notes

An I-node that fulfils aif:LackOfReliability_Desc can be added to the argument if the speaker proves untrustworthy. We then need an inference rule so that any I-node that fulfils aif:LackOfReliability_Desc also contradicts QUALIFIER.
