This is testing the application of argumentation to Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH), using the WW2 operation Fortitude South as an example.

Following the method...

Where will the main Allied invasion land?

Step 1 - Identify the possible hypotheses to be considered

Hypotheses:

Pas de Calais

The main invasion will land in Pas de Calais.

Normandy

The main invasion will land in Normandy.

Brittany

The main invasion will land in Brittany.

Cotentin Peninsula

The main invasion will land on the Cotentin Peninsula.

Elsewhere

The main invasion will land outside the range of close air support.

Assumptions:

Close air support

The invasion must have close air support.

Port needed

A major port will be needed to supply invasion forces.

Peninsula

Forces landing on a peninsula can be cut off on a narrow front.

Shortest crossing

The Pas de Calais affords the shortest crossing from Britain to mainland Europe.

Strongpoint

The Pas de Calais is the strongest point of the Axis defences.

Expansion

A landing site must offer opportunities for expansion.

Arguments

Elsewhere is ruled out by the need for close air support.

The risk of getting cut off on a peninsula is unacceptable, ruling out Brittany and the Cotentin Peninsula.
